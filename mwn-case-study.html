Chris Holm Product Designer, PSM I

Saving time by improving Healthcare CRM web app

Summary

Over the past two years, I have worked with multiple software dev teams to create many enhancements and features. The high-level goal is to improve workflows, improve business KPIs, and increase the speed of onboarding new customer service representatives.

Problems

Healthcare Coordinators were drowning in redundant tasks because the system they had just transitioned to was designed for another user group's workflow.

Outcomes

  • Improved the users' quality of life and workflow by removing redundant manual tasks and adding configurable flexibility to the system.
  • Task completion times improved by roughly 300%.

Users and their needs

Healthcare Coordinator Agents

  • Remove redundant tasks.
  • Create ways to complete multiple tasks with less clicks.
  • Change system workflow to match the users' workflow.

Constraints

We needed to work within the current tech stack and only involve changes to the frontend and APIs. No changes cou;d be made to the database.

Research, design, and prototyping needed to be completed within 2 sprints and needed to happen while juggling two other high-priority projects.

Process

Understand

Empathize, Define: The Healthcare Coordinators had transitioned to our new system for a few months and found that they were drowning in redundant tasks. During the initial project kickoff with the business stakeholders, the group identified that the MVP (Minimally Viable Product) solution, which IT initially built for a different user group's workflow, wasn't giving the Healthcare Coordinator group the KPI improvements that they had hoped. Define: We mapped the current state of the process and scenarios where frustrations took place.

Explore

Ideate: We did a "how might we" exercise to help document everyone's ideas for potential solutions to their frustrations. The group then selected the concepts that we wanted to investigate further.

Prototype: Two of the improvements just involved reusing existing functionality in other areas of the applications. For these updates, I created quick wireframes for the devs to work from so they could be implemented in the next sprint.

Prototype: For the more complicated implementations, I made proto-personas and mapped what the user flow could be. I made a prototype to give the stakeholder group something to interact with and give feedback.

Materialize

Test: I had a follow-up meeting with the dev teams, business stakeholders, and Healthcare Coordinators to validate the proposed changes. Except for a few minor changes, the consensus was that the solution would meet the needs of all groups.

Implement: I made the requested revisions and then worked with the dev team to start breaking down the work in the product backlog.

Understand

Empathize, Define: Once development was complete and released to production, we had bi-weekly meetings to get feedback from business operations stakeholders.
